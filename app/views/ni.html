Use this service to apply for £600 support to help pay your energy bills.

You will get this support automatically and do not need to apply if you:

  • have a standard electricity meter
  • pay your bills directly to your energy supplier (such as Power NI and SSE Airtricity)

You cannot apply if you’ve already had £600 automatically from your electricity supplier.

This is made up of £400 from the Energy Bills Support Scheme and a £200 Alternative Fuel Payment, regardless of how you heat your home.

Who should apply

Although most households in Northern Ireland get this support automatically, you may need to apply if your home:

  • has a communal electricity supply
  • has a non-domestic electricity connection
  • does not have a contract with an electricity supplier

This might mean that you pay for energy through a landlord, housing manager or site owner.

You can apply if:

  • you pay for the energy your household uses
  • you’re applying for your main, permanent home
  • you have a fixed address in Northern Ireland

Who cannot apply

You cannot apply for a discount if you’ve already had support from the Energy Bills Support Scheme and Alternative Fuel Payment or you live:

  • in purpose-built student accommodation
  • in a care home and do not pay some of your fees
  • on a boat with a continuous cruising licence
  • on a non-permanent caravan or mobile home site

Eligible homes

You may be eligible to apply if your main home is:

  • in a residential park home
  • on a boat on a permanent residential mooring
  • on a permanent Gypsy and Traveller site
  • part of a heat network without an electricity meter
  • in social or private rented accommodation which has a business energy connection or a communal electricity supply
  • off the mains electricity or gas grid
  • in a care home or assisted living facility and pay for some or all of your care
  • in a non-domestic property which is used as a home (such as a farm or a flat above a shop)

Check your eligibility and apply online

You will need:

  • an email address and phone number (if you have one)
  • your bank account details

If you rent your home, you’ll need to upload photo ID.

If you’re not registered for rates, you’ll need to upload a tenancy agreement or rates exemption certificate.

If you live in a care home, you’ll need to upload an invoice for your fees from the last 3 months.

If you’re applying on behalf of someone else, or for someone you have Power of Attorney for, answer the questions with their details rather than your own.

What you need to know

After you apply, BEIS and our delivery partner will check you live at the address you apply for.

You’ll be told about the progress of your application by email or letter. You should get a response within 4 weeks.

If you’re eligible, your council will arrange a one-off payment. You do not need to repay this money.

Other ways to apply

If you cannot apply online and need support, phone the Northern Ireland customer support team.
