{{ serviceName }} if you are not receiving it automatically

Applies to England, Scotland and Wales

Households in England, Scotland and Wales can apply to get support for household energy bills if you have not received it automatically.

Most people will have received these payments automatically.

You might be able to apply for:

  • £400 to help you with the rising cost of energy bills through the Energy Bills Support Scheme
  • £100 to help you with the rising cost of alternative fuels if you are off the mains gas grid

You might be eligible for one or both payments.

You can apply for energy bill support if:

  • you pay energy bills
  • your household has not received the payments automatically
  • you are applying for your main, permanent home

Students living in purpose-built student accommodation such as university halls of residence are not eligible to apply.

Who can apply for the £400 energy bills support

The Energy Bills Support Scheme is a £400 discount for eligible households to help with their energy bills over winter 2022 to 2023.

Most households will have received this automatically from their energy supplier as a monthly £66 or £67 discount to your energy bills.

You can apply for this payment if your home does not have a domestic energy connection and a direct relationship with an electricity supplier. This means that you pay for energy through your landlord, housing manager, or site owner.

  • in a residential park home
  • on a houseboat on a permanent residential mooring
  • on a permanent Gypsy and Traveller site
  • in a home on a heat network without an electricity meter
  • in social or private rented accommodation which has a non-domestic energy connection
  • in a home that is off the mains electricity or gas grid
  • in a care home or assisted living facility and pay for some or all of your care
  • in a non-domestic property which is used as a home such as a farm or a flat above a shop

Who can apply for the £100 alternative fuels payment

If you use alternative fuels which have been affected by rising prices to heat your home and are not connected to the mains gas grid, you are eligible for a £100 payment.

Most homes that are eligible for this payment have already received it automatically.

You might not have received this payment automatically if your home is in an area which is mainly connected to the gas grid or your home is not connected to either the gas or the electricity grid.

You are eligible for this payment if the main way you heat your home uses:

  • bottled gas
  • liquid petroleum gas
  • oil
  • bio oil
  • wood
  • solid fuel

If you live in a care home or supported accommodation

If you live in a care home or sheltered and supported accommodation and pay for some or part of your care fees you may also be eligible for one or both of these payments.

Your property manager should apply on your behalf and pass on the discount. Contact them to check if they have made the application on your behalf.

Before you start

The council tax bill payer from your household should apply for these payments. This is so that your local council can check you live at the address you are applying for.

If no one in your household has their name on a council tax bill, you will need to provide additional proof of address.

Examples of proof of address include:

  • a private or social housing tenancy agreement
  • a residential houseboat license

If you are applying for the £100 alternative fuel payment, you will need to provide proof that you use alternative fuels to heat your home in the form of a delivery note or receipt of purchase.
